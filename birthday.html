Chapter One

The Letter

Everything I could not say in one text message.

My love, happy birthday. Today you turn 20. Two decades of the world getting to experience you — your laugh, your softness, your strength, your beautiful, stubborn, tender heart. And I get the privilege of calling you mine.

I want to tell you something I need you to truly hear today: You are not lucky to be loved. You deserve it. Every single bit of it.

I have watched you carry so much this past year — exams, distance, hard days, uncertain nights. And you never stopped showing up. Not for me, not for the people you love, and not for yourself. That is extraordinary.

"Your 20s are going to be the decade where everything clicks."

Your 20s are going to be the decade where everything clicks. Where your dreams stop being "someday" and start becoming real. Where the things you have worked for quietly, consistently, in the background — finally arrive.

And I want to be there for every chapter of it. Not just the beautiful moments in Goa. Not just the late-night conversations. All of it. The ordinary Tuesdays. The hard months. The moments where you doubt yourself.

I am not going anywhere.

— Your Pune Boy, March 2026

20 Things I Love
About You

One reason for every year you have been alive.
